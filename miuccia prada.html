MIUCCIA PRADA

The Luxury-Fashion Pioneer

The Outstanding Moments and Achievement goes to Miuccia Prada:

  • 1950 | Miuccia Prada was born. She spent the last five years studying to became a doctorate in political science and performing mime at Milan's Teatro Piccolo,being a paid-up member of the Communist party and champion of women's rights in Seventies' Milan.
  • 1979 | She took over the Prada family business. Since sales were down, she expanded into luxury tote bags and backpacks in black with flat classic lines, made from nylon.
  • 1985 | Prada's first successful handbag design,a new line of black, unlabelled, hard-wearing, but finely-woven nylon handbags that immediately became must-haves for the fashion cognoscenti.
  • 1989 | She designed and introduced her first women’s ready-to-wear collection. The plain, almost austere lines of her designs provided a stark contrast to the overtly sensual designs of other labels of the time.
  • 1992 | The Miu Miu line was introduced as a less expensive womenswear line inspired by her personal wardrobe. She named it after her own nickname, Miu Miu. Clean and stylish designs, fine materials and exquisite craftsmanship.
  • 2014 | She become the co-CEO for Prada, together with her husband.
  • 2015 | The permanent headquarters of the Prada Foundation found their final home in a former industrial complex dating back to 1910 located in Largo Isarco, in Milan, which was transformed and reimagined by Rem Koolhaas, who turned it into the 19 thousand square meters of space that, today, host a rich program of exhibitions and cultural events.
  • 2016 | Miuccia Prada‘s pioneering approach also extends to architecture. She commissioned Pritzker Prize winners, Rem Koolhaas and Herzog & de Meuron, the creation of Prada’s “Epicenter” stores in New York, Tokyo and Los Angeles. The Foundation also opened “the Observatory” in the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II in Milan, a new location entirely dedicated to contemporary photography.
